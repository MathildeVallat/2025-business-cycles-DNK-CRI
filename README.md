# Business Cycle Analysis: Costa Rica and Denmark

An empirical and historical comparison of business cycles in Costa Rica and Denmark,
written as a Master's-level macroeconomics report by Andrés Fiallos and Mathilde Vallat.

---

## Overview

This report compares the business cycles of two small, open economies — Costa Rica
(upper-middle income) and Denmark (high income) — using the Hodrick-Prescott (HP) filter
and historical analysis. It examines volatility, persistence, and co-movement with GDP,
and concludes with tailored policy recommendations for each country.

## Methodology

- **HP filter** applied to log-transformed quarterly series with smoothing parameter
  λ = 1600 (standard for quarterly data)
- **Second moments** computed from the cyclical component: volatility (SD × 100),
  correlation with GDP, autocorrelation (lag 1), and relative volatility to GDP
- **Seasonal adjustment** applied to Costa Rica's employment series using the `seasonal`
  package (X-13ARIMA-SEATS)
- **National currency** used for second-moment analysis to avoid exchange rate distortions;
  **USD** used for historical figures to leverage Denmark's longer data series from 1960

---

## Key Findings

- Costa Rica exhibits higher volatility than Denmark across all variables, especially
  employment (4.09 vs. 1.01)
- Costa Rica's consumption-to-GDP relative volatility (1.19) exceeds Denmark's (0.85),
  suggesting limited consumption smoothing
- Denmark's public spending is slightly procyclical; Costa Rica's is slightly
  countercyclical
- Denmark shows higher persistence (autocorrelation) in employment and GDP than
  Costa Rica
